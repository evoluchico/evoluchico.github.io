Chico Q. Camargo

Postdoctoral Researcher in Data Science

Oxford Internet Institute, University of Oxford

Hi, I’m Chico!

I’m a Postdoctoral Researcher in Data Science at the Oxford Internet Institute, University of Oxford. I develop and apply computational tools to study collective human behaviour, focusing on public opinion dynamics, cultural evolution and urban mobility.

I graduated with a BSc from the University of São Paulo, as part of the Molecular Sciences Programme, and later worked on mathematical modelling in biology at the Wolfson Centre for Mathematical Biology, and at the Department of Zoology, University of Oxford. I then moved to a PhD (DPhil) in systems biology, also at Oxford, as a Clarendon Scholar in Brasenose College. In my PhD research, I used tools from complex systems and machine learning to investigate the physical principles that rule biological evolution.

Apart from doing science myself, I am also passionate about communicating it to wider audiences. I have spoken at science communication events such as FameLab UK and Oxford’s Science Cabaret, written for Huffpost Brasil, and am a part of the Portuguese-speaking YouTube educational channel BláBláLogia, which hosts videos on topics ranging from space travel to ecology to film making.
